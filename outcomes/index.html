Berkeley CBE PhD Outcomes Data

Project Summary

Brandon Curtis has been working with the College of Chemistry Development Office and the Graduation Division to gather job placement data on CBE PhD alumni. Several surveys collect different (incomplete) information and spread it across multiple databases in different university organizations, so this report attempts to summarize what IS known about alumni placement; future efforts will attempt to unify these datasets. A historical overview of where our graduates have gone could be useful for job search ideation, grad student recruiting, and lab selection purposes.

For detailed employment statistics by year and research group from the Campus Advancement Data System (CADS), see this spreadsheet !

Use 'Data → Filter Views → Temporary Filter View' to sort the data by degree year, research group, employer...

For a description of this and other alumni employment outcome datasets, see UC Berkeley - Graduate Alumni Outcomes in Google Drive.

For a broad overview of first-job employer type and geography, see the report below.

Graduate Division Report - Doctoral Placement Survey

The Doctoral Placement Survey attempts to assess the whereabouts of doctoral students after graduation. Each summer, paper forms are printed for each student who had graduated 12-18 months prior. The forms are circulated to Graduate Advisors, who determine the location of their graduates to complete the forms. If programs cannot locate their graduates, work-study students in the Graduate Division search for the graduates online and send followup emails to confirm identities.

Brandon's Summary

This dataset is limited, but very complete (95%+ of graduates). Note that this dataset explicitly lists the names of universities and national labs that received only one student, but (inexplicably) does NOT list the names of companies that received only one student. Current data covers alumni who graduated in the 15 years from 1998 to 2013.
