4. Students of Color Conference - Applications due 10/16 5pm

I would like to announce, on behalf of the ASUC, that the 27th Annual Students of Color Conference (SOCC) will be hosted on our campus for the first time since 2006! SOCC is the longest-running UCSA conference that focuses on students of color in higher education. Open to UC students from across the system, UC Berkeley will be welcoming over 900 students this November 6th-8th for this conference.

For students that are interested in applying to be part of the Berkeley Delegation for the conference, you can find the application at asuc.org/eavp. The DEADLINE to apply is Friday, October 16th at 5 PM.

For individuals that are interested in applying to host a workshop, lead an identity-caucus space, or perform at the conference, you can find those applications at asuc.org/eavp. The DEADLINE for those applicaitons is Friday, October 16th at 5 PM. Students, student groups, and campus staff are welcome to apply to host a workshop at the conference.

To stay up-to-date with the conference, be sure to "Like" our Facebook page for updates, which can be found here. If you have any questions, please be sure to contact socc@asuc.org.

Thank you and we look forward to seeing your applications!

5. Microaggressions in CBE

An important outcome of the recent town hall meeting was that the issue of microaggressions during faculty-student interactions was brought to the attention of faculty. In response to this, the faculty recently had a speaker attend one of their meetings to better explain microaggressions. It would be helpful, however, for GSAC to provide specific examples of microaggressions to the faculty. As has been stated earlier, such incidents may be conveyed to the faculty via GSAC through the anonymous comment box on the GSAC website. Comments made in this box are visible only to the GSAC presidents, and no identifying information is recorded. For more serious concerns that require response please see the OMBUDS office, or speak to the presidents, Carlet, or Nitash directly.

4.  Lab Shadowing Sign Up

Hey Second Years and Up,

We are looking for volunteers that would be willing to help first years choose a lab. Basically what we envision is that if you sign up, you will be someone that won't mind getting a cold email from a first year interested in your lab. After that is very flexible, but what we would like to encourage is allowing the first year to experience a larger portion of the day to day in your lab than what people have traditionally been shown. This can be anything from just talking more in depth with the interested student, to actually having that student shadow you as you run some experiment. We know that some experiments may seem boring to you and not worth showing, but that is exactly what we'd like to let people see. If you are open to doing this and are not already a lab rep, sign up here!

1. GLC special seminar: Research at Sandia National Laboratories

Neither Industry Nor Academia: Research at Sandia National Laboratories

Thursday, Sept. 3 11:00am, Pitzer Auditorium, 120 Latimer Hall

Speaker: Dr. Mitchell Anstey, Sandia National Laboratories

Sponsor: Department of Chemistry

Abstract: The common refrain among graduate students goes something like this: “so what are you doing after grad school? Industry or academia?” It’s an important question, but its simplicity betrays the complexity of the “real world” and the amazing options students have open to them. As a researcher at Sandia National Laboratories in Livermore, CA, I will relate my own experience in the national laboratory system and how the labs fit into our nation’s larger R&D structure, working with the application-driven ethic of industry while maintaining the freedom to explore fundamental science. My group’s work on redox flow battery electrolytes and spectroelectrochemistry will be discussed. I will also touch on the work of my colleagues, which spans inorganic, organic, polymer, and physical chemistry and materials science.

1. An opportunity to get involved with UC-wide student government (GA announcement)

Are you interested in meeting students from all UC campuses? Do you want to learn how to cultivate student power? Want to have a say in how your student leaders advocate on your behalf? Then you should sign-up to attend the University of California Student Association Congress!

The UCSA is the official student government recognized in the California State Constitution to represent the voice of the students on all 10 UC campuses.The UCSA lobbies the State legislature, engages in advocacy with the UC Regents and the Office of the President, and coordinates direct action when necessary to get student's needs met.

If you would like to be involved in the shaping of the UCSA's advocacy objectives this year PLEASE ATTEND UCSA CONGRESS. Congress is where students convene to share best practices, build community, and vote on the UCSA campaigns, which aim to maintain the quality, affordability, and accessibility of the UC.

Previous/on-going UCSA Graduate/Professional Student campaigns include JOBS! - a professional development campaign, and GrADE - a campaign to democratize graduate education.

The 2015 UCSA Congress will be held Aug 13-16 at the Irvine Marriott. If you are interested in attending please contact Iman Sylvain, the External Affairs Vice President of Graduate Assembly at isylvain@berkeley.edu or eavp-ga@berkeley.edu

All conference travel expenses will be covered by the GA.
