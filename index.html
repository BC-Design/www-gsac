Welcome to the GSAC Homepage!

Welcome to the website of the the Graduate Student Advisory Committee (GSAC) of the Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at University of California, Berkeley. Our goal is to improve the graduate life experience of students in this department. Please use the navigation above to explore our site and its resources.

Upcoming Events

GSAC Job Search Panel

Monday September 8, 5-6pm in Bixby North

Three senior grad students and one recent grad from CBE will be sharing their insights and answering your questions about how to navigate Berkeley career resources, sell your experience to companies, and ultimately obtain and evaluate a job offer. Even if you feel like graduation is a long way off, this is a great opportunity to start thinking about how you might set yourself up for the career you want when you leave Berkeley. Take advantage of the wisdom of those who have already been through the job search process! Informal discussion will continue after the panel at Free House.

First Fall ChemE keg

September 12, 5pm in Chem Plaza

Our first ChemE keg of the fall semester will be September 12th at 5pm. We will have grilled food, snacks, a keg of delicious beer, and hopefully some tailgating games!

Fall Student-invited Speaker

October 8, 4pm

Our Student-invited speaker series will continue this fall with a presentation by Dr. Brian Korgel from The University of Texas at Austin. The presentation will be followed by a wine and cheese reception. Talk title to come.

Annual Fall CBE picnic

October 18, Codornices Park

Our annual fall CBE picnic will be September 18th in Codornices Park. We will reserve the field along with picnic table areas so that we can have some sports games going the whole time.

Recent News

ChemE vs. Chemistry Homebrew Competition

August 1, 2014

We just had our 2nd annual ChemE vs. Chemistry Homebrew Competition with a great turnout from Chemistry and Chemical Engineering! Congratulations to the winners: 1st to Eric Sacia for his "Hopricot" IPA, 2nd to Cameron Rogers, Fiona Wilkes and Matt Zhou for their "Tanktop" Extra Pale Ale, and 3rd to Matt Youngblut for his "Vier Exen" Belgian Quad. Way to go homebrewers!
