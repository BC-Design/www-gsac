Welcome to the GSAC Homepage!

Welcome to the website of the the Graduate Student Advisory Committee (GSAC) of the Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at University of California, Berkeley. Our goal is to improve the graduate life experience of students in this department. Please use the navigation above to explore our site and its resources.

Upcoming Events

Career Seminars hosted by GSAC

This three-part seminar series will be given by Andrew Green, a PhD Career Counselor at the UCB Career Center. We will discuss career-related issues relevant to graduate students and it will be tailored to Chemical Engineering where possible. These talks are applicable to students in the Chemistry department as well and everyone is encouraged to attend. Coffee and cookies will be provided for each seminar.

Notes from each Career seminar will soon be uploaded to the Grad Student Info=>Career Development tab for those that could not attend or take notes.

Session 1: Exploring your Options, or 'I don't want to be my adviser!'

Tuesday, January 20, 4:30-5:30pm in 775 Tan Hall

A discussion of the wide variety of career options available outside the world of academia, and how to go about picking a satisfying career based on your interests.

Session 2: Building your Knowledge Base: Networking and Informational Interviews

Tuesday, January 27, 5-6pm in 775 Tan Hall

A discussion of how to network and build relationships with potential employers. Will also cover strategies for applying to jobs not present on campus through the career fair or info sessions.

Session 3: Converting your CV into an Effective Resume and Cover Letter

Tuesday, February 3, 5-6pm in Bixby North

A discussion of how to incorporate your graduate work into a resume and cover letter. This session will cover useful information whether you are converting a CV to a resume, or just updating an outdated resume with your new graduate work and experiences.

Prospective Student Visit Weekends!

February 6-7 and February 20-21

Prospective student visit weekends will be held the first and third weekends in February. These are a perfect time for prospective students to get to know the graduate students, professors, department, and area much better. Expect to meet a lot of people and have some fun!

Student-invited "Science and Society" Seminar

April 8,2015 4pm, Tan Hall

Our student-invited speaker for Spring 2015 will be Dr. John C. Berg of University of Washington. He is a professor of Chemical Engineering and a leader in the field of Interfacial and Colloid Science, having written a "bestselling" textbook titled "Interfacial and Colloid Science." Talk title to come.

ChemE vs. Chemistry Homebrew Competition

August 1, 2014

We just had our 2nd annual ChemE vs. Chemistry Homebrew Competition with a great turnout from Chemistry and Chemical Engineering! Congratulations to the winners: 1st to Eric Sacia for his "Hopricot" IPA, 2nd to Cameron Rogers, Fiona Wilkes and Matt Zhou for their "Tanktop" Extra Pale Ale, and 3rd to Matt Youngblut for his "Vier Exen" Belgian Quad. Way to go homebrewers!
